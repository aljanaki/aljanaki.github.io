Anna Aljanaki

Professor of Computational Methods in Time-Based Arts & Musicology

I work at the intersection of artificial intelligence, music perception, and computational creativity — with a focus on music emotion recognition, symbolic music analysis, generative music systems, and digital musicology.

anna.aljanaki@kug.at Google Scholar GitHub

Institute of Electronic Music and Acoustics · University of Music and Performing Arts Graz, Austria

Current Research

My broader research spans music emotion recognition, symbolic music analysis, generative music systems, and digital musicology — often in collaboration with ethnomusicologists and artists. I obtained my PhD from Utrecht University (2016) on machine learning methods applied to music emotion recognition, and subsequently held positions at the University of Geneva, Johannes Kepler University Linz, and the University of Tartu.

TemTa grant: NeuroNose

Detecting volatile biomarkers for neurodegenerative disease detection
Funded by the Estonian Research Council (TemTa grant, 2024). The project applies machine learning to mass spectrometry and sensor array data to identify olfactory biomarkers associated with neurodegenerative diseases.
